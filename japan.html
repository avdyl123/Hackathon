Japan has a rich cultural heritage, from traditional arts like calligraphy and tea ceremonies to festivals such as Gion Matsuri. Visitors are often amazed by the blend of ancient traditions and modern innovation in everyday life.

Japanese cuisine offers a variety of dishes, including sushi, ramen, tempura, and okonomiyaki. Each region has its local specialties, and food culture is deeply connected to the seasons.

When visiting Japan, it's important to follow local etiquette: bowing when greeting, removing shoes before entering homes, and maintaining quietness in public transport are common practices.
